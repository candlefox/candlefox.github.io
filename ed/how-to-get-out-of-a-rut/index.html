How-To: Get Out Of a Rut

by Olivia Blazevic
Browse Courses Today!

More often than not, we fail to realise when we are ‘stuck’ in a rut – these feelings generally form over time as you continue to undertake the same routines day-in, day-out.

When we continue to do the same things over and over with no intention, we eventually lose the drive that keeps us working towards something, which then slowly becomes replaced with the feeling of simply doing things to kill time and making us bored of life.

The realisation comes when these feelings transform into something absolutely intolerable, and the source of your dissatisfaction becomes crystal clear. It can go one of two ways: you’ll become stuck in a negative cycle, or you’ll break out of this rut and into a new direction.

Signs that you may be stuck in a rut

So, what exactly are these signs?

  • Every day feels like the same thing

    When you are stuck in a rut, your days begin to feel blur into one another, leaving you stuck with a loss of interest, dragging yourself through the motions.

  • You feel like you have to push yourself just to get through another day

    You find yourself focusing almost all of your energy in keeping yourself going until the day is over.

  • You lack inspiration

    When you choose to involve yourself in new endeavours or other tasks, despite your willingness to participate, your ideas bank is empty.

  • You have a lingering feeling of dissatisfaction

    You have that niggling feeling at the back of your mind telling you to try something new, but you don’t know what that ‘something new’ is, and your low mood and lack of motivation are preventing you from even starting.

  • You let the fear of the unknown consume you

    Yes, you know that taking the plunge and making this change will completely change your outlook on life, but you let being comfortable overcome any thoughts of taking that risk that may lead to transitional discomfort.

Why do we do things that we know won’t bring us the results that we desire?

  • The ‘Sunk-Cost’ Fallacy

    When you choose to focus on what you have already put into your journey, i.e. time, money, and energy, you can get caught up in the idea that these efforts haven’t gotten you very far, which can then influence your future drive.

  • Depending on ‘What-if's?’

    Yes, winning the lottery might solve all of your problems, but the likelihood of that happening is slim to none. This wishful thinking won’t drive any real change (unless you’re super lucky!).

  • Cloudy Judgement

    Sometimes, when we remain in a negative situation because we allow small sparks of positivity to raise our hopes that things might improve somewhere down the line. However, more often than not, this is not the case. Instead of being hopeful in a dead-end situation, take a step back and have a look at the big picture: is it really improving?

Why do we do things that we know won’t bring us the results that we desire?

1. Change up your routines

Change is hard – we are creatures of habit after all. Especially when we have adapted to the same daily routine, steering away from this can prove to be very difficult. However, when you do take that step and change up your routine, these new experiences, big or small, can be that breakthrough that gets you out of your rut.

Some things you can try:

  • Mix it up

    Try getting your morning coffee from the café down the road rather than your regular coffee joint. You could also try parking a little bit further from work and walk that little bit instead. This is definitely on the smaller scale of changeups, but the change of scenery will give you a fresh perspective and stimulate your mind a little more than your regular routine.

  • Talk to someone you don’t know

    This is a fantastic way of getting yourself out of your comfort zone, and an even better way to get yourself out there and expand your social connections.

  • Get out there!

    Set aside some time in the week to do something that you really enjoy – whether that be hanging out with friends, putting a bit more effort into that forgotten hobby or hitting the town and visiting somewhere you’ve never been.

2. Set realistic goals

When we finally realise we are in a rut, it can be overwhelming to think about what exactly keeps us in it and how we can escape it. A way to solve this is to break down your solution by setting manageable goals. It is important to remember that we often blame ourselves for failures that are circumstantial or out of our hands, or aim too high too quickly. The key to this approach is that you must be ruthlessly realistic about your abilities to achieve the goals that you have set. If your goal seems unreachable, take a step back and break them down even further.

3. Find your sense of purpose

This sounds like a heavy one, but it really doesn’t need to be. When you aren’t working towards anything, you are more susceptible to falling into a rut. When you have things to look forward to and determination to achieve something, you have the perfect formula for inspiration. The baby step for this approach is to perhaps organise something fun on the weekend that you can look forward to doing all week. A bigger one could be life goals that relate to changing up your career or establishing deeper connections with loved ones.

Some ways you can achieve this:

  • Acknowledge the little things that make you happy
  • Make plans, big or small
  • Volunteer your time

Getting out of a rut isn’t easy – that’s why getting stuck is such a common experience. When you do break out, however, you’ll realise just how rewarding it is.
